1860 Floyd County Census Please just use as a guide as human error has been known to happen 

This explanation is from a Perry County 1850 census but it is probably true of most counties.

Often times the enumerator did not walk up a hollow but instead asked the families that lived nearby the names and ages of those that lived in the hollow above them. Also many times they would count empty houses of families that had already moved from the county. In short they got paid by each name or household so they got as many households as possible. Most times they even counted people they met on the road as in the case of James Butler and Elijah Holliday who was peddlers.

That could be why there are so many conflicting ages between census years and names of people.
