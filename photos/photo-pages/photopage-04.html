Submitted by Lisa Anderson

 Nellie Lee Compton Meade & Albert Richard "Alb" Meade. Nellie was the daughter of John Wallace Compton & Ida Belle Adkins. She was born 22 February 1915 and passed away 31 March 2001. She is buried in the Albert Meade Family Cemetery, Hurricane Creek, Pike County, Kentucky, with her husband, Alb. Alb was born 22 July 1910 in Pike County, KY. He was the son of Alexander Meade and Lindy Stephens. Alb was a farmer and a coal miner, where he was crippled in a cave-in. He died on 7 April 1981. They had eight children.

Submitted by Judy Hager Stambaugh

 Sambo Nelson & Jane Grace Derossett Nelson  Sambo was the one shot of four at the Cannel Caol Gap, shooting, They were both from Prestonsburg, Ky. Those that died that night were Sambo Nelson, age 50, propretor of the beer joint, Fred Adams, 65 deputy sheriff. Willie Fannin 25, this was the man that started the Gun battle, his younger brother which died also was Hobart Fannin age 22. my grandmother was also shot in the arm. Fannin came there looking for a Sammons man, that had arrested him for something previously and Fannin held a grudge for this man. this was in the Floyd county times and Paintsville Hearld, Paintsville Hearld was 9-21- 21, 1939.

Sarah Elizabeth Bays, Granddaughter of Terry and Mattie Triplett of Garrett 

 Boy in wagon is Toby Osborne the s/o Mathias (Mack Osborne).  Man standing by wagon is another son, Granville B. Osborne.  Granville was married to Julia B. Haywood, shown standing next to him.  Standing next to Julia B. is her mother, Sara Jane Baldridge Haywood.  She is holding her granddaughter, Osie Osborne who later married Jeff Herald.  Osie was the mother of Edgar Herald of Langley & Juanita Clifton of Dwale.  Mack also had 4 other children not in the picture (Fleming, Biddy, Dora, & Dona).  2 unidentified granddaughters are shown on the right.  The man on the horse by the wagon is Robert M. Haywood, Julia's father.  In the extreme rear is Mathias C. Osborne & his wife, Catherine (Kate) Price Osborne, who came to Floyd County from Ashe County, NC in 1872 in a covered wagon.  This photograph was taken in 1896 on Spurlock Fork of Middle Creek.
