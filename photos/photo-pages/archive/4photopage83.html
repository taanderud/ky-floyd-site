 Sambo Nelson & Jane Grace Derossett Nelson Sambo was the one shot of fourat the Cannel Caol Gap, shooting, They were both from Prestonsburg, Ky. Those that diedthat night were Sambo Nelson, age 50, propretor of the beer joint, Fred Adams, 65 deputysheriff. Willie Fannin 25, this was the man that started the Gun battle, his younger brotherwhich died also was Hobart Fannin age 22. my grandmother was also shot in the arm.Fannin came there looking for a Sammons man, that had arrested him for something previouslyand Fannin held a grudge for this man. this was in the Floyd county times and PaintsvilleHearld, Paintsville Hearld was 9-21- 21, 1939.

Sarah Elizabeth Bays, Granddaughter of Terry and Mattie Triplett of Garrett

 Boy in wagon is Toby Osborne the s/o Mathias (Mack Osborne). Man standingby wagon is another son, Granville B. Osborne. Granville was married toJulia B. Haywood, shown standing next to him. Standing next to Julia B. is hermother, Sara Jane Baldridge Haywood. She is holding her granddaughter,Osie Osborne who later married Jeff Herald. Osie was the mother of Edgar Heraldof Langley & Juanita Clifton of Dwale. Mack also had 4 other children not in thepicture (Fleming, Biddy, Dora, & Dona). 2 unidentified granddaughters are shownon the right. The man on the horse by the wagon is Robert M. Haywood, Julia's father. In the extreme rear is Mathias C. Osborne & his wife, Catherine (Kate) Price Osborne, who came to Floyd County from Ashe County, NC in 1872 in acovered wagon. This photograph was taken in 1896 on Spurlock Fork of Middle Creek.
