County History

Floyd County was founded in 1797. On December 13, 1799, the Kentucky General Assembly passed legislation to form Floyd County as the 40th Kentucky county. The legislation became effective on June 1, 1800. The county was named for early pioneer and surveyor, James John Floyd.

The county was made from parts of Fleming, Montgomery, and Mason Counties. The county seat was Preston's Station, located on the Levisa Fork of the Big Sandy River. Later, it became Prestonsburg.

On April 8, 1808, a fire destorying all early records when the first court house burned down. The earliest records of government activity come from the year 1808. The first post office opened in 1816 as Floyd Court House.

Prestonsburg was used as a Confederate stronghold during the Civil War and two battles took place nearby: the Battle of Ivy Mountain on November 8, 1861 and the Battle of Middle Creek on January 10, 1862. Both were Union victories.

It contained all or part of Lawrence, Pike, Morgan, Breathitt, Letcher, Johnson, Rowan, Magoffin ,Wolfe, Elliott, Menifee, Martin, Knott and Perry . Final Boundary was formed in 1884.
