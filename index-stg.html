Floyd County, KY

Part of the KYGenWeb Family

Keeping genealogy records and resources free.

The county, founded in 1800, is named for Colonel John Floyd,
a pioneer surveyor and early Kentucky settler.

On December 13, 1799, the Kentucky General Assembly formed Floyd County as the 40th Kentucky county.
The county was made from parts of Fleming, Montgomery, and Mason County, Kentucky.
