2. JAMES ROWE2 SR. (ROWE1) was born 1753 in Culpepper Co., Virginia, and died 1830 in Pike Co. Ky.. He married ELIZABETH TRACEY 1791 in Giles Co., Virginia.

Notes for JAMES ROWE SR.:

James Rowe, Sr. left North Carolina in 1781 and appears in Botetourt

County, Virginia on the 1790 census along with Benjamin and George Rowe.

These men were probably brothers. Benjamin and George disappear from the

county after 1796. They could have moved to another state. James Sr.

remained in Botetourt until 1806 when he moved to Spruce Run, a branch of

New River in Giles County, Virginia. Stephen later lived on this land.

James Rowe, Sr. and all his sons except Stephen moved to Floyd County (now

Pike County) Kentucky, in 1817, and are on the 1820 and the 1830 census for

Floyd and Pike Counties, Kentucky.

He fought for the British in the Revolutionary War. He was wounded at the

Battle of the Hawk while fighting against the army of General "Lighthorse"

Lee, the father of Robert E. Lee. James was wounded by a sabre in the leg

and carried the wound the rest of his life.

3. STEPHEN3 ROWE (JAMES ROWE2 SR., ROWE1) was born 1780 in Virginia, and died 1843 in Giles Co., Virginia. He married BARBARA HUFFMAN April 04, 1801 in Giles Co., Virginia. She was born 1781 in Virginia.

Notes for STEPHEN ROWE:

Stephen lived in the vicinity of his father's home place all his life, as

evidenced by his purchase of land granted by his father in 1808 on Spruce

Run, a branch of New River. Stephen and Barbary lived in a log house, with

probably one room which most house's had at that time, they had a fireplace

in one end of the room, table, chairs and benches, the loom, as they had to

weave material for their clothing. They also had a spinning wheel in a

place of convenience as it was used constantly. The parents usually slept

in the main room and the children slept in a loft. Cooking was done over

the fireplace, which was also the heat source for the cabin.

Stephen bought and sold land several times between 1810 and 1837. I have

been unable to find the grave site for Stephen but I am sure he was buried

in Giles County, Virginia. Barbara, or Barbary which seemed to be her

nickname apparently moved to Pike County, Kentucky with her children as she

is listed on some of the documents in that county.

In the will of John Huffman, the father of Barbara Huffman Rowe, dated the

6th day of March, 1824 and recorded in the Clerk's Office of the Circuit

Court of Giles County at Giles August Court, 1834, contained the following

provision; "Negroes to be sold by my executors immediately after by decease

and the money arising from such sale to be equally divided among my

daughters Catherine Damewood, Sophia Roe, Barbara Roe, Faney Hevery and

Mary Bostevs except ten dollars to be give over and above to my daughter

Mary Boster, to be freely enjoyed by then forever."

Stephen may have married a second time in that Rebecca Rowe is listed as

the mother of Reuben

4. HUFFMAN4 ROWE (STEPHEN3, JAMES ROWE2 SR., ROWE1) was born 1829 in Giles Co., Virginia, and died November 09, 1863 in Richmond, Virginia. He married LOUISA STEELE January 21, 1850 in Pike Co. Ky..

Notes for HUFFMAN ROWE:

Huffman Rowe fought in the Civil War and mustered in at Peach Orchard,

Kentucky. He was a Private in the 39th Regiment, Kentucky, Infantry,

Company D on the Union side. While on scout duty in the head of Marrowbone

Creek in Pike County on August 24, 1863 he was captured by confederate

soldiers and taken to a rebel prison in Richmond, Virginia where he died on

November 9, 1863 of the typhoid fever at Hospital #21. Other Rowe men were

also members of the Regiment: Loyd Rowe, Ruebin H. Rowe, Sr., Ruebin H.

Rowe, Jr., Solomon Rowe, Jacob Rowe and William H. Rowe (son of Jonathan

Rowe).

Huffman first appears by name in Pike County, Kentucky in the 1850 census

as being married to Louisa and living next door to his mother Barbary. His

first name was taken from his mother's maiden name.

On November 24, 1852, from a Pike county land Grant of 1000 unimproved

acres to George N. Brown, Huffman bought 50 acres located on the first

right hand hollow of Marrowbone Creek. Later the land was a subject of a

Circuit Court action when Huffman's children turned 18 years old (1870's).

In a Pike county Court Order of 1852 he was appointed as surveyor of a

road: "Ordered that Huffman Rowe be appointed surveyor of the ----- road on

Russell Fork commencing at the Mouth of Marrowbone down the ----- to Guy

Powell's Mill and that he call on Jacob Blevins, Guy Rowe, John A. Rowe,

----- Adkins, Stephen -----, Piles Adkins to help keep the same in proper

--------------------.

More About HUFFMAN ROWE:

Cause of Death: Typhiod Fever contracted while a Prisoner of War at

Richmond, Virginia.

Medical Information: Fair complexion, dark hair and eyes.

Military service: November 15, 1862, Enlisted as a Private in Company "D"in

the 39th Regiment of Kentucky Mounted Infantry Volunteers.

Occupation: Farmer

Notes for LOUISA STEELE:

After Huffman's death in the Civil War Louisa applied for the children and

herself. However, Louisa could not read or write. When she and Huffman were

married the Justice of Peace, James Hamilton, mistakenly entered her name

on the marriage certificate as Malissa. The War Department denied her

claim. Only after numerous statements and documents were submitted proving

she was Huffman's widow did the War Department validate her claim. The War

Department granted her a pension of eight dollars per month plus two

dollars per month for each child under the age of sixteen back dated to

November 10, 1863.

It appears that Louisa's parents died when she was about four years old.

John Coleman was named her guardian and his name appears in her marriage

records.

More About LOUISA STEELE:

Note: Used both names Melissa and Louisa.

5. WILLIAM H.5 ROWE (HUFFMAN4, STEPHEN3, JAMES ROWE2 SR., ROWE1) was born January 16, 1847 in Lawrence Co., Ohio, and died May 19, 1925 in Lookout, Pike Co. Ky.. He married (1) SARAH E. PHILLIPS January 26, 1871 in Pike Co. Ky.. She died in Pike Co. Ky.. He married (2) DICY HALL 1915.

Notes for WILLIAM H. ROWE:

William was born in Lawrence County, Ohio. William is a twin to James and

was illegitimate. He was a Deacon at the Pine Grove Old Regular Baptist

Church in the late 1800's. The Church house is still in use at the same

location--Poor Bottom of Rockhouse in Pike county. Located at the back of

the church on the inner wall is a picture of three men identified as

Deacons. William "Bill" Rowe is one of these men. The Rowe's in Pike County

were mostly Old Regular Baptist, a part of the Southern Baptist.

According to the obituary of his brother, they moved to Pike County,

Kentucky when six (6) months old. William and James are first reported in

Pike County, census of 1850 in the household of Barbary Rowe (age 69) at

the age of 3. Also in the same household is Matilda Rowe 30, Francis Rowe

26, and a four month old male by the name of James S. Rowe. In a house next

door lived Huffman the son of Barbary, and his wife Louisa Rowe. In the

census of 1860 Matilda Rowe, William and James, who are now 13, are living

with Huffman and Louisa Rowe and three other children. Family tradition

reports the "a man by the name of Mays is their father, but William and

James were raised by a man named Rowe". I cannot prove or disapprove this

statement. However, I can reasonably state that Huffman Rowe was the man

that raised them. I cannot without doubt determine who is the mother of

William and James. In this report I have listed Louisa as the mother. At

least they were raised in her household.

Obituary:

Brother William Rowe was born January 16, 1647 and died May 19, 1925. He

married Sarah on January 26, 1871. To their union was born 10 children,

five still living. He joined the Pine Grove Old Regular Church in 1873. He

was soon afterwards made a Deacon and served faithfully in that capacity

until it pleased the Lord to take him from the shores of time, which was

near unto 50 years.

On Monday, the day before he died, he told the writer of a vision, in which

he said the Lord sent a messenger to show him the beautiful home to which

he was going, and he said he longed to go. And when one of his dons gave

him water he stated, "I want water from the fountain of living water".

About 30 minutes before he died he called his brother James Rowe, who had

been dead many years, and addressed him as a good brother, as if his

brother in spirit had come to him at the end of his weary pilgrimage to

accompany his spirit to his home beyond the skies. On Saturday before his

departure he called his children and step children and asked them to meet

him in Heaven. They all had a very high regard for their father, and their

loss is great, but we wish to say to them, "You should not sorrow as those

who have no hope, for while his body is sleeping beneath the sod, his soul

is resting in the sweet smiles of a crucified redeemer. He is no longer

present and no longer has to bear the hard trials of this world with which

you and I have to battle, in which he met so bravely. He said he would like

to see the Pine Grove Church come to peace, but that he would rather suffer

his head sundered from his body than have unclean spirits to enter her".

His body was laid to rest beneath the flowers of May beside his dear

companion who has been dead many years. Many friends attended. He was

married a second time to Dicy Hill, widow of William Hill, who is still

living.
