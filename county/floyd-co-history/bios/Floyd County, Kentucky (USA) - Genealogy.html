INTRODUCTION

     Floyd County is a county located in the U.S. state of Kentucky. It was formed in 1800. As of 2000, the population was 42,441. Its county seat is Prestonsburg. The county is named for Colonel John Floyd (1750-1783).

     On December 13, 1799, the Kentucky General Assembly passed legislation to form Floyd County as the 40th county of Kentucky. The county was made from parts of Fleming, Montgomery, and Mason County, Kentucky. The legislation became effective on June 1, 1800. The county seat was Preston's Station, later renamed Prestonsburg. The first court house burned down on April 8, 1808, destroying all the early records, so the earliest records of government activity come from the year 1808. Prestonsburg was used as a Confederate stronghold during the Civil War and two battles took place near by. The Battle of Ivy Mountain on November 8, 1861 and the Battle of Middle Creek on January 10, 1862. Both were Union victories.

     Counties adjacent to Floyd county are: Johnson County (north); Martin County (northeast); Pike County (east); Knott County (southwest); and Magoffin County (northwest).

BEAVER CREEK; BIG SANDY RIVER

Beaver Creek

LOCATION: Beaver Creek, 373650N-0824348W, is a stream that has three forks located in Floyd County, they are the Left Fork, Right Fork, and West Fork.

FAMILY HISTORY NOTES(s): Floyd County land records show several transactions for William Walkers eldest son Robert Walker and William Salisbury husband of Elizabeth Walker between 1826-30 most of them on Beaver Creek.

INTERNET WEB LINK(s): GNIS Feature Search

Big Sandy River

LOCATION:  The Big Sandy River, 382458N-0823545W,  flows on the Kentucky-West Virginia border, heads at the junction of Tug and Levisa Forks, flows north to the Ohio River at Catlettsburg.  The Levisa Fork, 380705N-0823606W.  Heads in Virginia, flows NNW into Kentucky to join Tug Fork to form the Big Sandy River at Louisa.

FAMILY HISTORY NOTES(s):  Robert Brown purchased 50 acres along the Big Sandy River on July 9, 1817.

A review of Floyd County Land Grants shows that the Levisa River was generally referred to as the Big Sandy River or Louisa river.  The Levisa river is actually a main tributary of the Big Sandy where they join at Louisa, KY about 35 miles north of
Prestonburg, KY.
